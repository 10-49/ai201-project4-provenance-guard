# Provenance Guard - Project Specification & Planning

## 1. System Design & Core Questions

### Detection Signals
utilize two signals to evaluate text. First signal handles the meaning and style, while the other looks at more structure behind writing mechanics.

1. **signal 1: LLM prompt-based classification**
   * **measures:** semantic patterns, predictability, and uniform tone.
   * **output format:** a float between 0.0 (100% confident human) and 1.0 (100% confident AI).
   * **functionality:** pass the text to groq with a system prompt telling it to analyze the style and return a single probability score as a JSON object.

2. **signal 2: stylometric heuristics**
   * **measures:** sentence length variance and vocabulary diversity (Type-Token Ratio).
   * **output format:** a float between 0.0 (highly variable, human-like) and 1.0 (highly uniform, AI-like).
   * **functionality:** split the text into sentences and words. compute the standard deviation of sentence lengths and then divide unique words by total words. a low sentence variance and a low vocabulary diversity push score closer to 1.0.

* **false pos vs false neg asymmetry:** 
  the system is designed to handle asymmetric risk, prioritizing the minimization of false positives over false negatives. this is done by the aggregation formula, it weights the semantic evaluation of the LLM heavier than the heuristics, preventing edge-case or odd presences in human writing from dominating the final classification.
  $$\text{Final AI Score} = (\text{LLM Score} \times 0.65) + (\text{Heuristic Score} \times 0.35)$$

---

### Uncertainty Representation
the system maps the **Final AI Score** to a confidence system. 

* **Calibration:**
  * **0.0 to 0.35:** likely human. the confidence score is calculated as:
    $$\text{Confidence} = 1.0 - \left(\frac{\text{Final AI Score}}{0.35}\right)$$
    
  * **0.35 to 0.65:** uncertain / the system is conflicted. The confidence score represents how close it is to the center:
    $$\text{Confidence} = 1.0 - \left(\frac{|\text{Final AI Score} - 0.5|}{0.15}\right)$$

  * **0.65 to 1.0:** likely AI. the confidence score scales upward:
    $$\text{Confidence} = \frac{\text{Final AI Score} - 0.65}{0.35}$$
    

a final confidence score of 0.6 in the uncertain tier means the signals are clashing, and the platform should explicitly tell the reader that the system isn't sure.

---

### Transparency Label Design
the frontend will fetch the exact string text generated by our backend based on the categories defined above.

* **Category 1: High-Confidence Human (Final AI Score < 0.35)**
  > "Written by a Human (Confidence: [X]%) — This content matches normal human writing patterns, showing natural stylistic and structural variety."
* **Category 2: Uncertain / Borderline (Final AI Score 0.35 to 0.65)**
  > "Uncertain — The system detected mixed signals. This text contains a blend of highly uniform structures and personal stylistic variations. Reader discretion advised."
* **Category 3: High-Confidence AI (Final AI Score > 0.65)**
  > "Generated by an AI (Confidence: [X]%) — This text exhibits heavy statistical markers, structural uniformity, and phrasing patterns typical of automated language models."

---

### Appeals Workflow
* **who can appeal:** Any logged-in creator using their `creator_id` matching a specific `content_id`.
* **what they provide:** The `content_id` and a text block named `creator_reasoning` explaining why the classification is wrong.
* **system Action:**
  1. the server catches the `POST /appeal` request.
  2. it looks up the row in our local SQLite database or JSON log using the `content_id`.
  3. it changes the `status` field from `"classified"` to `"under_review"`.
  4. it appends the `appeal_reasoning` directly into the record.
* **Human Reviewer Queue View:** a human administrator calling an internal dashboard or reviewing logs will see a filtered table displaying:
  `Timestamp | Content ID | Creator ID | AI Score | Original Label | Creator Reasoning`

---

### Anticipated Edge Cases
1. **short-form sentence / poetry:** A human writing a poem with highly repetitive refrains, short uniform lines, and simple words. The stylometric engine will see zero sentence variance and flag it as a `1.0` (AI), potentially triggering an incorrect "Uncertain" or "Likely AI" label.
2. **technical/academic style:** A human writing a dry, formal paper about technical subjects. The language is naturally structured, rigid, formal, andor predictable. The Groq LLM may flag it as AI due to the lack of casual slang or erratic thoughts typical of human writing.

## 2. Architecture


FLOW 1: SUBMISSION
[Client] 
   │ (POST /submit: text, creator_id)
   ▼
[API Endpoint] ───► [Signal 1: Groq LLM] 
   │                     │ (LLM Score)
   │                     ▼
   ├──────────────► [Signal 2: Stylometrics]
   │                     │ (Heuristic Score)
   ▼                     ▼
[Confidence Scoring Engine] 
   │ (Combined Score 0.0 - 1.0)
   ▼
[Transparency Label Generator]
   │ (Selects text variant based on thresholds)
   ▼
[Audit Log (SQLite/JSON)] ◄─── (Writes record)
   │
   ▼
[JSON Response] ──► [Client]


FLOW 2: APPEAL
[Client]
   │ (POST /appeal: content_id, reasoning)
   ▼
[API Endpoint]
   │
   ▼
[Database / Storage] ──► (Updates status to 'under_review')
   │
   ▼
[Audit Log] ◄─── (Appends appeal reasoning to original record)
   │
   ▼
[JSON Response] ──► [Client]
